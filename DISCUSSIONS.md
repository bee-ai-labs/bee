# Discussions 💬

[GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/bee-ai-labs/bee/discussions) is the town square of the hive —
where we ask questions, share what we've built, propose ideas, and help each other learn. This
page explains how to use it well.

## When to use Discussions vs. Issues

| Use **Discussions** for… | Use **Issues** for… |
|---|---|
| Open-ended questions ("how would I…?") | Concrete, actionable problems ("this link is broken") |
| Sharing a project or article you made | A specific bug to fix |
| Proposing an idea before it's a plan | A well-scoped task ready to be worked |
| "Which approach is better?" debates | Content that's factually wrong |

Rule of thumb: **if there's a clear, mergeable action, it's an Issue. If it's a conversation,
it's a Discussion.**

## Categories

| Category | For |
|----------|-----|
| 📣 **Announcements** | Releases, milestones, community news (maintainers post) |
| 🙏 **Q&A** | Ask anything about AI engineering or using Bee — answers get marked ✅ |
| 💡 **Ideas** | Propose new content, examples, or features |
| 🙌 **Show & Tell** | Share what you built with what you learned here |
| 🗺️ **Learning Help** | "I'm stuck on this concept / path — help me understand" |
| 🛠️ **Contributing** | Coordinate on bigger contributions, find collaborators |
| 💬 **General** | Everything else |

## How to ask a great question (Q&A)

The better the question, the better — and faster — the answer:

1. **Search first.** Someone may have already asked.
2. **State your goal**, not just your blocker.
3. **Show what you tried** — code, commands, or the page you followed.
4. **Include specifics** — exact error text, OS, Python version, which example/page.
5. **Mark the answer** ✅ when you get it, so the next person benefits.

## Community norms

- 💛 **Be kind.** Everyone was a beginner once. See the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- 🔁 **Pay it forward.** Answer a question while you wait for yours.
- 🎯 **Stay on topic.** AI engineering, learning, and building — that's the hive.
- 🙅 **No spam or self-promotion** unrelated to learning/building AI.

## From Discussion to contribution

Great discussions often become great content:

- A well-answered Q&A → a new **FAQ** entry or a short guide.
- A popular Idea → a roadmap item and a `[WANTED]` marker.
- A Show & Tell → inspiration (or the basis, with permission) for an example.

If your discussion could become content, say so — and consider
[contributing it](CONTRIBUTING.md) yourself!
